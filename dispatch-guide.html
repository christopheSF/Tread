Dispatch Guide

How a job gets from your dispatch board to your driver's phone — the board, assigning to your own drivers or a vendor, and the step-by-step assign → send → monitor flow.

Part 1

Dispatch Overview

Dispatching is the daily act of getting work onto the right driver's phone. You can dispatch from the web dashboard or the mobile app — assign, send, and monitor jobs on either surface — and your drivers receive, accept, and start those jobs on their phones.

Tip

New to Tread? Set up your trucks, drivers, and users first — see Set Up Trucks & Drivers. Then come back here to dispatch.

What you dispatch

A Project is the container (a multi-day scope of work); an Order is the daily slice you actually dispatch. You assign and send the jobs inside an order.

ConceptRole in dispatch
ProjectHolds the customer, sites, materials, and rates
OrderThe day's work: trucks requested, start time, target loads/hours
JobOne truck's assignment within an order — what you send to a driver

The dispatch view

The Dispatch view (on web, and the Dispatch tab on mobile) is your command center for the day. Each order card shows its status, truck count, and the per-job rows you act on — assign a driver, send the job, and track its status.

Each job moves along this status flow as the day runs:

Accepted→To Pickup→At Pickup→Loaded→To Dropoff→At Dropoff→Unloaded→In Review→Completed

Assign to your own driver — or a vendor

When you assign a job, you choose who hauls it. In the Assign panel you toggle between Driver and Vendor — that choice is the dispatch mode. Pick the wrong one and the driver never sees the load, or the wrong company gets billed, so confirm the relationship first.

ModeWho hauls itIn the Assign panelWhen to use
RegularYour own driverDriver tabYour own employee drivers and trucks
DirectAn Owner-Operator you dispatch directlyDriver tabA single-truck hauler whose truck you assign yourself
HotA hauling company that sub-dispatchesVendor tabYou hired a multi-truck vendor; their dispatcher picks the driver
Warning

Vendor (Hot) dispatch needs an accepted Vendor connection, and a Direct Owner-Operator must be shared to you — otherwise the job won't send or the driver won't appear in your Assign list. Check Settings → Vendors and Settings → Drivers first.

Before you dispatch — checklist

  1. Work exists: a project and at least one order for the day.
  2. Trucks & drivers are set up: drivers added (and invited so they have the app), trucks created, and default trucks paired to drivers.
  3. Connections are confirmed (if using vendors): for Hot/Direct dispatch, the Vendor connection is accepted and any Owner-Operator drivers are shared to you.
Part 2

Dispatch a Job (Web & Mobile)

Dispatching works on both surfaces. Assign, configure, send, and monitor jobs from the web dashboard, or dispatch on the go from the mobile app. Either way, your driver receives and starts the job on their phone.

Note

The numbered markers on each screenshot correspond to the numbered steps next to them.

Dispatch from the web

Step 1 · Assign a driver (or a vendor)

Click Assign on the job row to open the panel, then choose who hauls the job.

  1. Assign — opens the Driver / Vendor picker on the job row.
  2. Driver — assign one of your own drivers (Regular), or an Owner-Operator you dispatch directly (Direct). Search by name, phone, or truck number.
  3. Vendor — hand the job to a hauling company that will sub-dispatch it to their own driver (Hot).

Step 2 · Bulk assign to a vendor (optional)

To give many jobs to one hauling company at once:

  1. Click the Bulk Assign (persons) icon at the top right to open the Assign to dialog.
  2. Use + to set the number of trucks for the selected vendor.
  3. Click Assign All to assign every selected job to that vendor at once.

Step 3 · Configure the job details

Hover a row and click Edit to open the Edit Job screen. Confirm the pickup and drop-off sites, the truck and trailer, the start date and time, and the job's rates. Click Submit when the details are correct to save the job.

Step 4 · Send the jobs

Click Send on a single row to dispatch that driver, or Send All Jobs at the top to send every pending assignment at once. Drivers receive a push notification immediately.

Warning

“Send All Jobs” dispatches every job with an assignment. Double-check the list before clicking — drivers are notified instantly.

Step 5 · Monitor the jobs

Follow each job's Status as the day runs. You can manually advance a status by clicking the down arrow in the status box, and follow trucks on the Live Map.

Accepted→To Pickup→At Pickup→Loaded→To Dropoff→At Dropoff→Unloaded→In Review→Completed

Dispatch from mobile

You can run the same dispatch from the Tread mobile app — useful when you're away from your desk. Open the Dispatch tab to see the day's orders.

  1. Open the order & tap + Assign — tap the ▼ arrow next to the order's truck count to see the assigned trucks, then tap + Assign to start adding a driver.
  2. Pick a driver — search by name or truck number and tap to select.
  3. Send the job — tap Send. The driver gets a push notification immediately and can accept and start the job from their phone.
Note

You'll see the driver's location on the map as soon as they start the job.

On mobile — your driver receives

Once you send a job, the assigned driver gets it on their phone. Here's what they do:

1

View the request

New offers appear in the driver's Assigned tab under New Tread Requests. The driver taps View Request to see the job details.

2

Accept the job

The driver reviews the pickup, drop-off, and material, then taps Accept (or Decline). Until they respond, you'll see their status as Pending on your board.

3

Start the job

The driver taps Start to begin the run. Their location now appears on your Live Map, and the job's status starts moving through the flow.

Tips & common pitfalls

Warning

Assign to the right place. Use the Driver tab for your own drivers and Vendor for haulers who sub-dispatch. Picking the wrong one is the most common reason a driver never receives a load.

  • Driver didn't get the load? Check the job status — if it's stuck at Assigned (not Sent), you still need to hit Send. If it's sent but the driver shows Pending, they haven't accepted yet, or they may not have the app or location permissions.
  • Vendor (Hot) jobs show the driver's name on your board only after the vendor's dispatcher accepts and assigns one of their drivers.
  • Set a default truck on each driver so it pre-fills on every assignment — fewer clicks, fewer mistakes.
