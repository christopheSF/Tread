Setting Up a Company Hierarchy in Tread

A step-by-step guide for Tread CSMs and Ops team members. Covers how to structure a new customer account — connecting customers, adding vendors, and inviting users — so the company can dispatch, manage jobs, and see the right data.

CSM Reference Settings → Customers Settings → Vendors Connect with Tread ID Dispatch as switcher

How the hierarchy works

🏢 Hauler (Your Company)
e.g. Trans-Phos · Tread ID: 96BAA7
👤 Customers
Companies you haul for
🚛 Vendors
Subhaulers you dispatch to
Hauler is the primary company — the one paying for Tread. This is the company you create the account for.

Customers are the companies the hauler hauls for. They can be connected (they have their own Tread account and can see their jobs) or flat (internal reference only).

Vendors are subhaulers the hauler dispatches to. They receive job assignments via the Tread driver/vendor app.

Once connected, a user can switch context using the "Dispatch as" dropdown at the top right — letting CSMs or admins view the account as a specific company.
1
Open the customer's account in Tread
Settings → User Profile to confirm you're in the right company

Before making any changes, confirm you are dispatching as the correct hauler company. Check the "Dispatch as" label in the top-right header — it shows which company's context you are currently working in.

If you need to switch to the customer's account, use the dropdown to select the right company.

Settings overview — the left sidebar shows all configuration sections. "Dispatch as: Staging Hauler Co" is visible in the top-right header.
Tip: The "Dispatch as" dropdown only appears once you have access to more than one company. If you need to access a customer's account and don't see it in the switcher, ask Ops to add your user email to that account.
2
Add Customer Accounts
Settings → Customers

Navigate to Settings → Customers. This is where you manage all companies that the hauler hauls for. There are two ways to add a customer:

🔗 Connect (existing Tread account)

Use this when the customer already has a Tread account. Enter their Tread ID (visible in their top-right account badge) to link the two companies. Connected customers can log in and see their own jobs.

➕ Create Account (flat / new)

Use this when the customer is new to Tread or doesn't need their own login. Creates a "flat" customer record used for internal reference and job tracking. Can be upgraded to connected later.

Settings → Customers — the "+ Connect" and "+ Create Account" buttons are in the top-right of the grid.

Clicking Connect opens a dialog where you enter the customer's Tread ID. This creates a bi-directional relationship — the customer company can see orders dispatched to them.

"Connect with Tread ID" dialog — enter the customer's 6-character Tread ID to link their account. Their Tread ID is visible in the top-right badge on their Tread login.
Note: To find a company's Tread ID, ask them to look at the top-right corner of their Tread account — it shows their 6-character ID (e.g. 96BAA7). Tread Support can also look it up.
  1. 1Go to Settings → Customers
  2. 2Click + Connect if the customer already has a Tread account — enter their Tread ID and click Search, then Connect
  3. 3Click + Create Account if they are new — fill in account name, external ID, and any other details
  4. 4Repeat for each customer company that needs to be linked to this hauler
3
Add Vendors / Subhaulers
Settings → Vendors

Navigate to Settings → Vendors. Vendors are subhaulers the hauler dispatches jobs to. Once added, the hauler can assign vendor drivers to jobs from the Dispatch page.

Vendors are invited via email or SMS — they receive a Tread invitation and set up their own company account and drivers. Once accepted, they appear in the vendor list and their drivers become available for dispatch assignment.

Settings → Vendors — shows all connected vendor/subhauler companies. Use the invite button to send a Tread invitation to a new vendor.
  1. 1Go to Settings → Vendors
  2. 2Click Invite Vendor — enter the vendor company name and contact email or phone number
  3. 3The vendor receives an invitation via email or SMS to create their Tread account
  4. 4Once accepted, the vendor appears in the list and their drivers can be assigned to dispatched jobs
  5. 5For the vendor notification template to send, refer to the Onboarding Chain document
If a vendor already exists in Tread under a different connection (e.g. connected to another hauler), they will still need to accept a new invitation to connect to this hauler. Each hauler–vendor relationship is separate.
4
Invite Users to the Hauler Account
Settings → User Profile (or via the Users page)

Users are the people who can log in and operate Tread on behalf of the hauler company — dispatchers, billers, foremen, admins. Each user gets a role that controls what they can see and do.

Available Roles

Company Admin — full access
Dispatcher — create and manage orders
Biller — approve tickets and billing
Foreman — approve loads on-site
Reporting — read-only reports access

CSM Internal Access

To access a customer's account for support, a Tread team member email (e.g. [name]@tread.io) is added as a Company Admin user on the account. This allows the CSM to log in and switch context using "Dispatch as".

  1. 1Go to Settings → User Profile (your own profile) or navigate to the Users section
  2. 2Invite each user by email — they receive a Tread invitation link
  3. 3Assign the appropriate role(s) for each user
  4. 4For CSM access: add the relevant @tread.io email as Company Admin so the account appears in their "Dispatch as" switcher
Tip: A user can have multiple roles on the same account (e.g. Company Admin + Biller + Dispatcher). Assign all roles that match their responsibilities at onboarding.
5
Verify the hierarchy with "Dispatch as"
Top-right header → Dispatch as dropdown

Once customers and vendors are connected and users are invited, use the "Dispatch as" dropdown in the top-right header to verify the setup by switching between company contexts.

When you select a company from the dropdown, the entire Tread UI switches to show that company's data — their projects, orders, drivers, settings, and reports. This is how CSMs access a customer's account without impersonation.

  1. 1Click the Dispatch as dropdown in the top-right corner of any page
  2. 2Select the customer company from the list
  3. 3Navigate to Settings → Customers and Vendors to confirm the linked companies appear correctly
  4. 4Navigate to Dispatch to confirm drivers and orders are visible for this company
  5. 5Switch back to your own company when done
Note: The "Dispatch as" dropdown is only visible once your user account has been added to at least one additional company. New CSMs will need their email added to each customer they support.
