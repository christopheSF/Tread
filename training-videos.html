Script
0:00 Before you create your first project, you need to load your master data — the building blocks Tread uses to auto-fill forms and match rates. Head to Settings.
0:12 Start with Users — invite everyone who'll be using Tread. They'll get an email to activate their account and download the driver app if needed.
0:24 Next, Equipment Types — this is critical. Create your truck categories first — Dump Truck, Tri-Axle, End Dump — then add your actual trucks underneath each type. Order matters here.
0:42 Then Materials, Customer Accounts, and Sites. For sites, you can draw a geofence polygon directly on the map — Tread uses that to automatically capture loads when a truck enters and exits.
1:05 Got a spreadsheet of data? Use the bulk import CSV templates in each section — they'll save you hours for larger fleets. Once your data is in, you're ready to build your first project.
Key talking points
Upload order matters: Users → Equipment Types → Equipment → Materials → Customer Accounts → Sites.
Geofence polygons on sites enable automatic load capture — no manual entry by drivers.
Bulk CSV import is available for every data type — recommend for fleets with 10+ trucks.
🎬 Production tip: Show the Settings sidebar and hover over each section name as you mention it. You don't need to click into every section — just show it exists. Finish by zooming into a site with a geofence polygon drawn on the map.

Script
0:00 Let's create your first project. From the Projects tab, click New Project.
0:06 Give your project a name — this is the name your team will search for every day, so make it recognisable. Then select the Customer Account, your start date, and your pickup and dropoff sites.
0:22 Projects also have a Project External ID — if your accounting system has a job number, put it here to make billing reconciliation seamless.
0:32 Scroll down to Freight Rates. This is where you set your customer rate — what you bill — and your vendor and driver rates — what you pay. Add conditions like equipment type or material to create rate tiers that auto-match to jobs.
0:58 You can add as many rate cards as you need. Tread will automatically match each job to the most specific rate that fits. Hit Submit and your project is live.
Key talking points
Project External ID = your accounting job number. Set it now to avoid manual reconciliation later.
Freight Rates cascade to all orders under this project — change the project rate and all open orders update automatically.
Rate matching is automatic: most conditions wins. A "Sand + Dump Truck" rate beats a generic "Base Rate".
🎬 Production tip: Pre-populate the dialog with real-looking data before recording (company name, realistic rates). Scrolling through a blank form looks less polished than one with data already typed in. Zoom into the Freight Rates accordion when explaining conditions.

Script
0:00 Once your project is ready, head to Dispatch and click New Order. This is what your team will do every morning.
0:10 Select your project — watch how the rates, sites, and materials auto-fill from the project. You just need to set the date, how many trucks you need, and the job quantity. Tread handles the rest.
0:28 One thing to get right: the job quantity unit. Are you tracking by tons, loads, or hours? Pick the right one here — it drives how loads are counted and how billing calculates at the end of the day.
0:44 Hit Submit. Your order is live and jobs appear in the dispatch board — one card per truck slot. You can assign drivers one-by-one, or click Bulk Assign to fill the entire order from your driver pool at once.
1:05 Click Send All and drivers get an instant push notification on the Tread mobile app. They accept, tap Start, and their location goes live.
1:20 Jump to Live Map to see your trucks moving in real time — load counts, cycle times, and ETA all update automatically as each driver completes a run.
Key talking points
Job quantity UOM (tons / loads / hours) is the most common setup mistake — it can't be changed after jobs start without affecting billing.
Bulk Assign + Send All is the fast path for experienced dispatchers running 20+ trucks.
Drivers receive the job details, pickup/dropoff addresses, and rate card on their phone automatically.
🎬 Production tip: This is the most important video — spend the most polish here. Highlight the auto-fill moment when you select a project (rates + sites populating) with a brief pause. The Live Map ending is visually impactful — end on a map with trucks visible if possible.

Script
0:00 If you use subcontractors, Tread has a full vendor management flow. Go to the Admin tab.
0:08 Add a vendor company — they'll receive an email invite to create their own Tread account. Once they're in, they can manage their own drivers and trucks. You don't need to handle their data.
0:24 For your own drivers, just enter their name and phone number — they'll get an SMS with a link to download the Tread driver app. The app is where they accept jobs, submit scale tickets, and log hours.
0:42 One important note: phone numbers must be unique across the whole platform. If a driver's number is already in Tread from a previous employer, the invite will conflict. Contact support and we'll resolve it quickly.
1:00 The sub-dispatch flow works like this: you assign a job to a vendor — the vendor then assigns it to one of their drivers — and you see the full chain in your dispatch board. Rates are kept separate at each level automatically.
Key talking points
Phone number conflicts are the #1 onboarding blocker — confirm driver numbers with vendors before go-live.
Vendors manage their own drivers and trucks inside their Tread sub-account — you only see their jobs, not their internal data.
Share the Vendor Setup Guide and Driver Guide with each vendor at invite time.
🎬 Production tip: Emphasise the phone number warning visually — maybe add a brief red overlay or annotation. This single point prevents the most common support tickets during onboarding week.

Script
0:00 After your trucks have run for the day, everything lands in Approvals — your quality gate before billing. There are three sections: Approve Work, Approve Jobs, and Tickets.
0:16 Start with Tickets. Every scale ticket a driver submitted shows up here. Tread's AI has already reviewed them for anomalies — flagging tickets where the weight, timestamp, or material looks off. Review the flags, make any corrections, and approve.
0:40 Approve Work handles driver shift hours. You'll see each driver's start and end time, total hours, and any breaks. If a driver forgot to clock out, you can manually adjust the time here before approving.
1:00 For larger teams, use bulk approve — select all completed shifts and approve in one click. Tread won't let you bulk-approve anything flagged, so you're always reviewing exceptions individually.
1:20 Once everything's approved, that data is locked in and ready for billing. No double-entry, no spreadsheets — your billing team picks up exactly where Approvals left off.
Key talking points
AI ticket review is on by default — it catches the most common billing errors before they become disputes.
Approve Work = shift-level hours. Approve Jobs = billable hours by job/phase code. Both feed into billing separately.
Approved data is immutable — if a correction is needed after approval, it goes through a formal adjustment flow with an audit trail.
🎬 Production tip: Show a ticket with an AI flag highlighted (red/yellow indicator) before showing the approval action. The contrast between "flagged" and "approved" states tells the story visually without needing to explain the AI.

Script
0:00 Once approvals are done, billing is fast. Tread splits billing into two sides: Receivables — what your customers owe you — and Payables — what you owe your drivers and vendors.
0:16 To create a Receivable invoice, select the completed jobs — Tread pre-fills the rate, quantity, and any add-ons or fuel surcharges from the approved data. Review the line items, add any manual adjustments, and save.
0:38 For Payables, you create a settlement that batches all driver and vendor payments for a period. The vendor rate and driver rate from each job pre-populate automatically — no spreadsheet required.
1:00 Add any fuel surcharges or deductions, review the totals, and finalise the settlement. Each driver and vendor gets their own line showing exactly what they're owed and why.
1:20 Finally, export your invoice as a PDF to email to your customer, or if you're connected to QuickBooks, Sage, or Dynamics 365 — click sync and the invoice goes directly into your accounting system. No re-entry, no copy-paste.
Key talking points
All rates, quantities, and add-ons on the invoice come directly from approved data — the only manual step is review and finalise.
Fuel surcharge (FSC) can be set as a % or flat rate per invoice and applied automatically to all qualifying line items.
If an accounting integration is connected, invoices sync in real time — no batch exports needed.
🎬 Production tip: This video has the highest ROI for billing personas — they often don't see Tread until go-live week. Show the invoice line items clearly and zoom in on the "Total" row before exporting. Ending on the sync-to-accounting moment is a strong close.
