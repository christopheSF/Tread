High PriorityMissing: Required vs Optional fields — the #1 onboarding question
In Igel session #8, Dalton asked "is drop-off required?", "is material required?", and "do I need a vendor rate?" across the same 40-minute call. The answer is always "not required but recommended for reporting" — but this is never documented. Customers are guessing or asking CSMs.
📍 Source: George Igel Onboarding #8 (May 28) — multiple explicit questions
Suggestion: Add a "Required vs Recommended" table to the Create Project/Order page and the Onboarding guide. Two columns: what happens if you skip it, and what reporting you lose. One table, permanent reference.
High PriorityMissing: "Running" jobs — on-site hauling with no fixed load target
The Running checkbox came up in both Igel sessions. Tim recommended setting it at the project level so dispatchers never forget. But there's no documentation explaining what "Running" mode does differently: geofence triggers still fire, loads still accumulate, but the job doesn't auto-end after hitting the truck load target. This is critical for on-site earthmoving jobs.
📍 Source: George Igel #7 (May 27) and #8 (May 28)
Suggestion: Add a "Running vs Fixed Load" explainer to the Orders & Projects core concept. Include: what it means, when to use it, and Tim's recommendation to set it at the project level. A single callout box would cover 80% of confusion.
High PriorityMissing: Project → Order → Job → Ticket flow diagram on landing page
The video's central message — "project is the template, order is the daily instance, job is the driver assignment" — was also restated verbally by Tim in the Igel sessions. Customers who read the docs before onboarding don't understand the cascade, and CSMs repeat this explanation on every call.
📍 Source: Loom video (core theme) + George Igel #7 (Tim restated 3×)
Suggestion: Add a Project → Order → Job → Ticket → Approval flow diagram as the first element on docs.tread.ai, before the four entry-point cards. Even a text-based version removes the need for CSMs to explain this on every kickoff call.
New from CallsMissing: Child Account vs Internal Trucks — the fleet architecture decision
George Igel spent 20+ minutes debating whether their subsidiary (DBM dump trucks) should be a child Tread account or internal drivers. The trade-off: child = better for future separate dispatcher, but back-office approval requires toggling accounts. Internal = simpler dispatch, but visible to everyone. This decision affects the entire account setup and should be made before onboarding begins.
📍 Source: George Igel #7 (May 27) — extensive discussion
Suggestion: Add a "Fleet Architecture Decision" guide to the Onboarding section. Decision tree: Are these trucks owned by a different legal entity? Do you need a separate dispatcher for them? Will they have customers outside your account? → Internal vs Child vs Vendor.
New from CallsMissing: Vendor onboarding playbook and communication templates
Reynaga had one week to onboard 10 vendor companies and 30+ drivers before a June 8 go-live. Latefa had to manually provide a communication template for Andrea to text to her vendors. This template doesn't appear to exist as a self-serve resource in the docs — every CSM creates it ad hoc. For brokers especially, mass vendor onboarding is the hardest part of launch.
📍 Source: Reynaga Transportation onboarding (May 29)
Suggestion: Create a "Vendor Onboarding Playbook" page with: (1) sample text/email template to send to vendors, (2) what vendors need to do before first dispatch, (3) how to bulk-import vs. invite individually. Include Tread support number in the template. This removes a CSM-dependent step entirely.
New from CallsMissing: How to test the driver and foreman experience yourself
Dalton Zemba (Igel) asked directly: "Can I create a separate account with my personal email to test as a foreman?" Tim answered yes, and recommended it — but noted company admins see extra tabs in the mobile app that regular foremen don't. This is an important nuance for dispatcher-admins doing UAT. Not documented anywhere.
📍 Source: George Igel #8 (May 28)
Suggestion: Add a "Testing & UAT" section to the Onboarding guide: how to create a secondary test account with a personal email or phone number, assign it Foreman role, and test the mobile workflow — without seeing admin-only tabs that normal drivers and foremen don't see.
Medium PriorityMissing: Job state machine (Pending → Active → In Review → Completed)
The video ends with the driver clicking Submit and the job going to Completed — but the Completed → Approved transition (where billing feeds from) is skipped. Foreman approval flows are in Specialized Workflows, but the job status model isn't explained as a concept anywhere.
📍 Source: Loom video (chapter 3) + implied across all sessions
Suggestion: Add a "Job Lifecycle" reference page showing every status: Requested → Accepted → En Route → atPickup → onSite → In Review → Completed → Approved → Settled. One diagram, permanent reference for both dispatchers and drivers.
Medium PriorityMissing: "Day in the Life" end-to-end narrative for dispatchers
Impact's onboarding planning call revealed that Sameer structures training into 5 modules delivered sequentially. But the docs don't have a single page that narrates one complete day of dispatching — from morning project→order creation, through driver assignments and geofencing, to afternoon approvals. Customers who read the docs in isolation miss the connective tissue.
📍 Source: Impact Sand & Gravel planning (June 1) + implicit across all sessions
Suggestion: Add a "A Day in Dispatch" workflow guide that follows a single haul from start to finish. Link to the relevant detailed sections at each step. This is the missing narrative layer that orients new dispatchers before they dive into individual concept pages.
Lower PriorityEnhancement: Sub-dispatch / broker model not prominent enough
Reynaga is a pure broker — no owned fleet. Their entire setup revolves around sub-dispatch (direct-to-driver) and vendor management. The docs mention sub-dispatch in specialized workflows but don't have a dedicated "Broker Mode" guide showing how the workflow differs from a fleet owner's.
📍 Source: Reynaga Transportation onboarding (May 29)
Suggestion: Add a broker-specific variant to the workflow guides — or add a "Your industry segment" selector on the Quickstart page that surfaces the broker-specific flow (vendor invites → sub-dispatch → approvals) separately from the fleet-owner flow.
